“Tail Dependence of Consumer Debt Defaults: A Non-parametric Vine Copula Approach”
File Size: 444 kb
File Type: pdf
 Download paper
Effective assessment on the default risk of consumer debts is critical for decisions ranging from portfolio risk management to macroeconomic policy making. This paper studies the risk of default in four major consumer debt categories -- mortgages, credit card, line of credit, and installment loans in Canada from 2009 to 2018. Stylized facts on the default of consumer debts are documented. A non-parametric simplified copula model is implemented to estimate the tail dependence of defaults in different loan categories. The economic implication of these tail dependence is illustrated with a value-at-risk (VaR) analysis on a loan portfolio in which the portfolio weights mimic the aggregate ones in the macro economy.
